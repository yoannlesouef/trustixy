## Problem Section

**Section title:** The EU AI Act is not a one-time checkbox.

Most companies treat AI compliance like a document exercise: generate a PDF, file it away, move on. That approach breaks down the moment a regulation updates, a system changes, or an auditor asks for your history.

**Three pain points (icons + short text):**

**No structured inventory**
You probably use AI tools across teams — a sales chatbot here, a scoring model there. Without a central registry, you can't even answer "which AI systems does our organization use?"

**No audit trail**
A compliance document without history is worth little to a regulator. When was the system last assessed? Under which version of the regulation? Who validated it? These questions need answers.

**Regulations change — and no one tells you**
The EU AI Act is a living regulation. When new guidelines are published, which of your systems are affected? Without active monitoring, you won't know until it's too late.

**Section title:** Designed for auditability.

When a prescriber co-signs, they confirm they have reviewed the classification and obligations using their professional judgment. The co-signature scope is explicitly defined in the partner agreement — it is a professional review, not a legal guarantee.
